OVERVIEW

The CADG dataset is a new dataset for Crowd Anomaly detection from Drone and Ground views. The dataset provides a sophisticated simulation of how people would behave during an incident or accident in a public space, enabling the evaluation of various crowd activity detection methods under the real-world conditions. The dataset is not only the largest and most comprehensive dataset currently available for crowd activity detection, but also provides video captured from both a moving drone and fixed cameras.

We organized the activities into normal and four key crowd anomaly activities, assuming surveillance in a real-world public space i.e., (i) fight, (ii) theft, (iii) illness, and (iv) dispersion, as shown in the following examples. All videos were captured in a gymnasium with up to 12 actors. The normal activities include a wide variety of daily activities such as random walking, standing, texting while walking, and talking with several people. For the crowd anomaly activities, a few randomly selected people played particularly important roles, such as people suddenly falling ill or robbers. The rest of the actors intervened by, for example, walking around while paying attention, stopping to observe the incident, or talking to the afflicted or victims.
